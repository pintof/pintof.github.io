Thailand By Land

Nature

From volcanoes of millenia bygone, to extensions of the Himalayan Foothills, and Limestone peaks carved out by the valleys, canyons, and caves below, the North of Thailand is a mountaineer's paradise. With it's diverse landscapes however, every nature lover is sure to find their raison d'etre. Tree huggers will appreciate the lush jungles & forests that cover a quarter of the entire country (and are also the oldest evergreen rainforests in the world), the coastline protecting mangroves, and the 200 different floral species per hectare making this one of the most bio-diverse plant areas on the planet. Birdwatchers will glee trying to spot the multiple endagered species hosted here from a collection of nearly 1000 native & migratory bird species, including birds of prey. Cold blooded enthusiasts can enjoy being slithered on by the nearly 200 species of snakes which include Cobras, Pythons, and Vipers. Other reptiles include amphibious tortoises (like the Asian Giant Tortoise which can live over a 100 years), and over 300 lizard species. Fellow warm blooded lovers will appreciate the nearly 300 species of mammals here which make up 10% of the world's animals. Theres everything from majestic elephants, to fierce tigers. Playful primates like monkeys, Gibbons, and Macaques. Exotic animals you've never head of like Sun Bears & Sambars. And even edible animals! Like deer & wild hog for the avid hunter.

Cities

From colourful Tom Yum soups to colourful Ladyboys, Thailand's cities offer spicy cuisines for the bold of heart. Thai ciusine is world famous for it's spicy, tangy, sweet, and bold flavours that derive their taste directly from herbs & aromatic leaves as opposed to traditionally used spices. Fun fact - the bold flavored drink Red Bull was actually invented in Thailand. Thai cities also offer live entertainment from feirce Muay Thai fights to red light district Ping Pong shows both of which originated in Thailand, and show the vigour of humans pushing their physical limits. Being in a populous dense area such as the city can be particularly fun during three Thai festivals that are so popular, they are internationally known. Full Moon Party is the wildest rave around, the Lantern Festival lights up the entire sky with specks of light, and Songkran (Water Festival) turns the entire country into a water fight battleground for 3 whole days! Cities also offer fun activities like sightseeing tours, traitional Thai cooking classes, and Eco Tourism featuring a range of eco friendly activities unique to the area like learning how to build houses from natural materials like clay & bamboo. On the outskirts of Thai cities exist many ancient temples dating back to the 11th century. Designed with stunning & intricate Buddhist architecture, with a touch of Hindu influence, these structures will satiate the palate of the Renaissance Man seeking history, art, culture, and architecture.
